3C's of Game Design

Software Used: Unreal Engine 4
Languages Used: Blueprints, C++

Overview

The 3C's of Game Design is an important aspect of games and is used by many in the game development industry to describe the complex, interdependent relationship between the inputs of the player and the reactions of their character in the game, and how that is captured in the frame of the game's camera. As such, these projects are dedicated to understand more about the 3C's of Game Design and an attempt to execute them within a game to create a better player experience. I have thus far explored on biped and quadruped characters, and also a vehicle. Everything in these projects were made from scratch (i.e. no plugins, code components or templates from the marketplace were used) except for the assets. These projects were also made as a foundation to future games I might be making for game jams. Therefore, the components of the systems in these projects can be easily modified to suite the needs of various games.

Vehicles

I wanted to create a physics based vehicle pawn that behaves very similar to vehicles in real life. There are many racing games in the market that have such behaviours and one such example from the games I have played recently is Forza Horizon 4. Using that game as an inspiration and reference, I tried to replicate some of the components that can be found in actual cars so that the player can have an experience that is similar to driving them.

The vehicle in this project is a pawn that has several components such as the engine, gearbox, differential, suspension, clutch and brakes working together to function the car. The vehicle pawn and its components are highly customisable. The mesh of the vehicle and wheels can be changed with ease. The vehicle can be selected to be manual or automatic. It can also be configured to be front wheel drive, rear wheel drive and all-wheel-drive. Apart from these, the vehicle specifications (e.g. gearbox ratio, suspension etc) can be also be configured to match the specifications of its actual counterpart. I have enabled camera lag and camera rotation lag to create a smooth camera movement when the vehicle speeds up, slows down and drifts.

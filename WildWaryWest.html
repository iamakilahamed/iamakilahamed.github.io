Wild Wary West

Project Type: University group project (CS4350 Game Development Project)
Project Duration: ~10 weeks
Software Used: Unreal Engine 4, Blender, Figma, Visual Studios, GitLab
Languages Used: Blueprints, C++
Primary Role(s): Lead Gameplay Programmer/Designer

Overview

Wild Wary West is an asymmetric online party game of teamwork and betrayal where the fate of the Wild Wary West lies in the hands of the finest sheriffs ever known.

Wild Wary West was showcased at the NUS Open House 2023.

Download Wild Wary West.

Summary of my contributions

I took the role of Lead Gameplay Programmer/Designer, implementing most of the player's functionalities. My duties included:

  • Player spawning with unique costume and role allocation
  • Player movement
  • Camera
  • Combat
  • Interactions with objects
  • Camouflage ability
  • Ensuring relevant mechanics are replicated
Overall, I spent my time between scripting in C++ and blueprints, and collaborating with teammates. As this was my first time working on a online multiplayer game, the key takeaway was the knowledge I gained about networking in games and design consideration for multiplayer games.
